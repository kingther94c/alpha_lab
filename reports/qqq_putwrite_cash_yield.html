Selection rule was frozen before opening the holdout: require positive excess CAGR in both discovery and validation, cap worst pre-holdout drawdown at 20%, then maximize the weaker split's excess CAGR. This is a balanced-risk definition of “最合适”; it is not the full-sample highest-return cell.

Practical paper-trading start: keep the selected 25Δ / 42 trading-day / 126-day recovery rule, but scale collateral from the model frontier's 75% to 50% of NAV. This is a conservative two-thirds risk haircut for option-chain, margin and proxy uncertainty—not a second policy selected on holdout performance. The 75% line remains the audited model headline so the haircut is visible rather than rewritten into history.
  • Cash sleeve: use a 1–3 month T-bill/box-spread total-return proxy net of 19.49 bp/year. For operations, SGOV/T-bills may be cleaner collateral than BOXX because broker margin treatment is account-specific.
  • Entry timing: strike and filters use prior-close information; trade is booked at the next close and earns no same-bar return.
  • Assignment: stop writing new puts; hold QQQ until price reaches premium-adjusted breakeven or the time limit, then sell and wait one day before the next write.
  • Current model gate: No trend gate in the selected policy. This is illustrative; live advice must ingest executable bid/ask and broker buying-power rules.
Operational caveat. “Cash invested in BOXX” is not identical to cash collateral. The broker can haircut BOXX, raise house margin during stress, or require liquidation before assignment settlement. Keep a liquidity buffer and confirm collateral eligibility in writing.

3 · Strategy policy comparison / IV 选择与接股退出

Apples-to-apples setup: every row uses 25Δ, 42 trading-day puts, 50% collateral, the same calibrated premium proxy and cash hurdle. Only entry selection or assigned-stock exit changes. The simple baseline always writes when flat, then exits assigned QQQ at premium-adjusted breakeven or 126 trading days.

Pre-holdout winner: Breakeven AND VXN ≤25% OR 126d had a worst discovery/validation excess CAGR of 3.6%. In the sealed holdout it lagged the simple baseline by 1.6% (2.5% versus 4.1% excess CAGR). This is exactly why the holdout is shown rather than quietly retuning the rule.

Best IV-entry rule by pre-holdout rank: VXN − 21d RV ≥ 5pp. It produced 3.8% holdout excess CAGR and -6.0% max drawdown, versus baseline 4.1% and -7.5%. Selective IV generally reduced trade count and tail exposure, but aggressive thresholds often gave up too much premium time.

8 · Assumptions & failure modes

  • Return source: T-bill/box carry + volatility risk premium. The put leg is paid for warehousing crash risk; assignment is not a free repair mechanism.
  • Data: adjusted QQQ/SPY closes and VXN/VIX from Yahoo Finance; DTB3 from FRED; no historical QQQ chain.
  • Option model: European Black–Scholes; target strike from prior-close delta; $1 strike grid; daily theoretical mark; calibrated IV multiplier; bid haircut plus $0.65/contract.
  • Cash: prior observed DTB3 accrued across calendar-day gaps, less BOXX's current net expense ratio. Tax treatment, distributions, spreads and broker haircuts are excluded.
  • Assignment: close-based expiry proxy; QQQ shares use adjusted prices; early exercise and dividend timing omitted.
  • Tail risk: a crash followed by a slow recovery leaves the sleeve holding QQQ and forgoing fresh premium. The max-hold rule realizes losses; removing it merely hides them.
  • Capacity: QQQ options are liquid, but OTM bid/ask widens sharply in stress. Whole-contract sizing makes small accounts lumpy; collateral fraction must be calculated after rounding.

9 · Productionization path / 生产化路径

  1. Ingest a live QQQ chain with timestamp, bid/ask, IV, delta, DTE, open interest and volume; reject stale/wide quotes.
  2. Use build_put_write_advice only as the sizing shell; pass the real bid and broker-specific collateral haircut.
  3. Persist advisory decisions and next-day executable quotes for 3–6 months. Compare model premium, quoted bid and realized fill.
  4. Add alerts, not autonomous trading: assignment probability, buying-power buffer, BOXX/SGOV liquidity, and timeout-state monitoring.
  5. Only after paper validation, build an execution adapter under quant_bot_manager; retain the existing live-money gates.
